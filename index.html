Going On 30

Using an arbitrary number as real motivation.

I was talking to a friend of mine. He mentioned that, he had just turned thirty, and that forced him to re-evaluate his life, and led to him making a big life decision.

This shocked me. He was my age. Oh no wait. He was a little older. A year and change. So that means very soon, I too would be turning thirty.

Wow. Okay. I get that it's just a number. But it still is definitely something that forces you to reconsider things.

So I made what I consider to be a very wise decision. Let me preempt the sudden dawning of a realization, and rather, use it as a driving force.

A list.

Let me create a list of all the things that I want to do before I turn thirty. Lets take that deadline, and turn it into a target.

I wanted to take my last year of my twenties and make it the best year of my twenties.

Set a bunch of goals for myself that I can truly be proud of achieving.

The List

The list was kept secret until the thirties arrived. I had made goals previously where just telling people about the goal, and hearing nice things was enough dopamine, and I never ended up completing the task.
This wouldn't be one of those times.
